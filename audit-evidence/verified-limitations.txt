  statement: The cost-benefit tool's `risk_adjusted_revenue_musd` output and its "Risk-adjusted revenue" printout are actually risk-adjusted gross profit, since the value is multiplied by an 0.80 gross-margin factor (cad/cost_benefit.py:87), so the benefit-cost ratio compares profit to cost despite the "revenue" label.
  evidence:  cad/cost_benefit.py:86-87 (gross_margin=0.80; risk_adj_rev = loa * peak_annual_rev * years_at_peak * gross_margin), 109 (key 'risk_adjusted_revenue_musd'), 139 (printed 'Risk-adjusted revenue')

The mislabel is real, not overstated; gross_margin is also listed in assumptions (line 120) so the calculation is transparent, but the field/print names remain inaccurate. No external spec is involved.

[CONFIRMED] No time-value discounting over a ~9.5-year horizon inflates the benefit/cost ratio
  statement: The cost-benefit model values future peak-sales gross profit at face value with no NPV/discount-rate parameter, even though sales begin only after summed phase durations of ~9.5 years from preclinical, so its benefit/cost ratio overstates a properly time-discounted rNPV.
  evidence:  cad/cost_benefit.py:87 (risk_adj_rev = loa * peak_annual_rev * years_at_peak * gross_margin — no discount term); cad/cost_benefit.py:45-51 (phase durations sum to 9.5 yrs before launch); cad/cost_benefit.py:90 (bcr = risk_adj_rev / cost_dollars); no discount-rate flag in argparse at lines 152-165
  note:      Independently confirmed by reading the whole file. Line 87 applies loa, years_at_peak, and an 0.80 gross margin but no discount factor; line 90 divides this by undiscounted remaining cost. There is no discount/NPV flag in main()'s argparse (lines 152-165). Minor refinement to the audit's wording: gross_margin is applied so the figure is gross profit (not raw revenue), and the cost side is also undiscounted — but because revenue is realized years after spend, omitting discounting still inflates BCR, so the claim stands.

[CONFIRMED] Asymmetric risk treatment: revenue is probability-weighted, cost is not
  statement: In the cost-benefit triage, revenue is risk-adjusted by multiplying by the likelihood of approval while the "expected remaining cost" is the full deterministic sum of all remaining per-phase costs (scaled only by a modality multiplier), with no probability-of-reaching-phase weighting and no time discounting on either side; so benefit and cost are risk-adjusted on inconsistent bases and the benefit/cost ratio is not a true rNPV/expected-value (it tends to be conservative, since probability-weighting later costs would lower expected cost).
  evidence:  cad/cost_benefit.py:81 (exp_cost = sum of all remaining phase costs * mult, no LOA weighting, no discount) vs cad/cost_benefit.py:87 (risk_adj_rev = loa * peak_annual_rev * years_at_peak * gross_margin); result key labeled "expected_remaining_cost_musd" at line 106 and docstring "expected remaining development cost" at line 8.
  note:      Verified directly in cad/cost_benefit.py. The audit's technical observation is accurate: line 87 weights revenue by LOA, lines 80-81 sum the full remaining cost path unweighted and undiscounted. Worth a small correction to the audit's framing: this asymmetry biases the BCR downward (conservative), not upward, because a proper rNPV would weight later-phase costs by the (sub-1) probability of reaching them, reducing expected cost and raising the ratio. The file's own docstring already disclaims it as a "rough go/no-go triage aid... NOT financial advice, a valuation" (lines 13-14), but the "expected remaining cost" label (lines 8, 106) does overstate what is actually a full conditional forward-path cost.

[CONFIRMED] Drug-likeness score double-counts Lipinski space (QED + Ro5)
  statement: The drug-likeness score linearly combines QED (0.6) with a Ro5-violation term (0.4), so Lipinski properties (MW, logP, H-bond donors/acceptors) that QED already incorporates are partly counted a second time; the 0.34-per-violation penalty and the pChEMBL/11 potency cap are hardcoded design constants, not fitted parameters, and the code explicitly labels the whole score a transparent, non-validated triage heuristic rather than an efficacy prediction.
  evidence:  cad/virtual_triage.py:239 (return round(0.6 * qed_part + 0.4 * ro5_part, 3)); docstring line 234 "QED + Lipinski Ro5 compliance"; ro5_part penalty line 238 (max(0.0, 1.0 - 0.34 * ro5)); pChEMBL cap line 244 (min(pchembl, 11.0) / 11.0); non-validated disclaimer lines 228-232
  note:      Confirmed: the math in the file matches the audit and the property overlap between QED and the separate Ro5 term is real. There is no spec being violated (SPEC MISMATCH false is correct) — it is a deliberate heuristic. Severity is partly mitigated by lines 228-232, which already disclose the weights/penalties/cap are non-validated design choices, so this is a known-and-flagged limitation rather than a hidden bug. A README note should keep that mitigation in view and avoid implying the score claims predictive validity.

[CONFIRMED] Box center (mean of atoms) and box size (min/max extent) are computed on inconsistent bases
  statement: In cad/binding_site.py the docking-box center is the arithmetic mean of the ligand atom coordinates (line 81) while the size is derived from the (max-min) extent plus 2x padding (line 82), so the box is centered on the atom centroid rather than the bounding-box midpoint (max+min)/2 and is therefore not symmetric about the ligand's true extent; the 8 Angstrom pad normally absorbs this offset, but for a skewed atom distribution on an axis spanning more than 16 Angstrom the centroid can sit far enough from the midpoint that the box clips part of the ligand.
  evidence:  cad/binding_site.py:81 (center = mean of coords) and cad/binding_site.py:82 (size = (max-min)+2*pad)
  note:      Factual reading in the audit is accurate (center=mean at line 81, size=extent+2*pad at line 82; Vina treats the box as center +/- size/2). The reference-point inconsistency is genuine. The clipping consequence is correctly hedged: it is only possible when |mean - midpoint| > pad(8A), which mathematically requires the axis span (max-min) > 16A AND a strongly one-sided atom distribution. In practice the centroid of a real ligand is close to its midpoint and the 8A pad almost always compensates, so this is a latent design inconsistency / edge-case risk rather than a routine failure. The cited line range in the claim (8-9) refers to the docstring describing the tool's purpose; the actual computation is lines 79-83.

[CONFIRMED] 8 Angstrom per-side padding (16 Angstrom per axis) is a hardcoded constant, large for focused re-docking
  statement: The docking box is the full co-crystal ligand min-max extent plus a fixed 8 Å padding per side (a hardcoded default, 16 Å added to every axis, so a ~10 Å ligand yields a ~26 Å box), derived solely from ligand extent rather than detected pocket geometry; this is larger than the tighter boxes typically recommended for redocking and should be narrowed or verified for focused docking runs.
  evidence:  cad/binding_site.py:79 (pad: float = 8.0 default) and cad/binding_site.py:82 (size = (max-min) + 2*pad, i.e. +16 Å/axis); fpocket/P2Rank are referenced not here but in cad/dock.py:15,162 and cad/run_pipeline.py:256
  note:      Confirmed on the math: pad=8.0 default (line 79) and 2*pad=16 Å added per axis (line 82). The README/CLI string at line 122 ("ligand envelope + 8 Å padding") is itself accurate (8 Å per side), so there is no code-vs-doc spec mismatch — the audit's point is a defensible design critique that 8 Å/side is oversized for redocking, which is consistent with standard Vina guidance favoring tighter boxes. The fpocket/P2Rank references cited by the audit are in dock.py/run_pipeline.py, not in binding_site.py's docstring, but the audit's substantive claim (pad is a constant, not pocket-derived) holds.

[CONFIRMED] Docking box ligand is the largest hetero group, not necessarily the co-crystal inhibitor
  statement: The docking box is centered on whichever single non-solvent hetero residue has the most atoms (cad/binding_site.py:74); since the IGNORE blocklist (lines 31-35) excludes only water, ions, and common cryo-additives — not cofactors (e.g. HEME/FAD/NAD), glycans, lipids, or detergents — on a cofactor- or additive-bound structure the box can land on a non-inhibitor group, and the tool never verifies that the chosen ligand occupies the pocket where the triaged hits would bind.
  evidence:  cad/binding_site.py:74 (max-atoms selection); cad/binding_site.py:31-35 (IGNORE set omits glycans/cofactors/lipids); cad/run_pipeline.py:156-157 and README.md:118 (described as "co-crystal ligand envelope")
  note:      Confirmed. The "largest hetero group" claim and the missing pocket-correspondence check are both real. One refinement: residues are grouped per (res, chain, seq) (line 69), so a multi-residue glycan tree is split into separate small groups rather than counted as one — the audit's "sugar tree" example is therefore unlikely to win, but a single large cofactor residue (HEME ~43 atoms, FAD ~53, NAD ~44) plausibly can outrank a small inhibitor, so the limitation stands. Note also the code already prints an inline warning at binding_site.py:124, but the README.md:118 and provenance strings (run_pipeline.py:156-157) state it as fact without the caveat.
