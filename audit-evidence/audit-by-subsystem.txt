# RECOVERED AUDIT — per-dimension


## Economic / feasibility model (cad/cost_benefit.py + scoring in cad/virtual_triage.py)

**Summary:** The feasibility model is a transparent deterministic spreadsheet, not a predictive model. Its clinical-phase approval priors (cad/cost_benefit.py:38-41) and clinical-phase costs (cad/cost_benefit.py:47-49) are genuinely and correctly transcribed from the cited BIO/Informa CDSR 2011-2020 and DiMasi 2016 figures — that part is real and well-attributed. But the surrounding machinery — the preclinical LOA, the "filed" cost, modality multipliers, the oncology factor, gross margin, and every verdict threshold — are invented heuristics presented inside the same output block under a single "sources" line that implies provenance they do not have. The "probability_of_approval" is literally a static modality/phase/oncology lookup multiplied together; it carries zero target-specific or molecule-specific signal, yet it is emitted per-"proposal" and labeled a feasibility verdict, inviting a target-specific reading. The benefit side mislabels risk-adjusted gross profit as "revenue," applies probability weighting to revenue but not to cost (asymmetric risk treatment), and uses no discounting over a ~9.5-year horizon, all of which inflate the benefit/cost ratio. The triage score weights are explicitly flagged non-validated, which is honest, but QED and Ro5 are combined in a way that double-counts Lipinski-space.

**Usefulness:** Useful today only as a transparent, editable order-of-magnitude planning calculator for a portfolio/BD analyst who already understands rNPV and treats the output as a sanity-check sketch. It correctly reproduces published BIO phase success rates and DiMasi phase costs, so it can ballpark 'is this program in the right zip code.' It would mislead anyone who reads probability_of_approval as a forecast for their specific target or molecule (it is identical for all programs at a given phase/modality), who treats the BCR as a valuation (no discounting, profit mislabeled as revenue, cost not risk-weighted), or who trusts the preclinical 6% and the favorable/unfavorable thresholds as sourced (they are invented). It is not usable by oncologists or for any clinical decision, and med-chemists should note the triage drug-likeness score double-weights Lipinski properties. The honest in-code disclaimers help, but the single 'sources' line on the output blends sourced and invented numbers without distinction.

**README notes:** Any public limitations section should state plainly: (1) Only the clinical-phase LOAs (Phase I/II/III/Filed, cost_benefit.py:38-41) and clinical-phase costs (cost_benefit.py:47-49) come from the cited BIO and DiMasi sources; the preclinical approval rate, 'filed' cost, durations, modality multipliers, oncology factor, gross margin, and all verdict thresholds are unsourced heuristics. The 'sources: BIO/Informa CDSR 2011-2020; Wong et al. 2019; DiMasi et al. 2016' string (cost_benefit.py:121) should not be presented as covering the whole assumption block. (2) probability_of_approval is a modality/phase benchmark identical for every program at that phase — it is NOT a target- or molecule-specific prediction; the word 'verdict' overclaims. (3) The benefit/cost ratio is undiscounted (no NPV over a ~9.5-year horizon), risk-weights revenue but not cost, and the 'risk-adjusted revenue' field is actually gross profit (x0.80 margin) — so the ratio is not a valuation. (4) The triage drug-likeness score combines QED and Ro5, which overlap, so Lipinski properties are partly double-counted; weights are non-validated (already disclosed at virtual_triage.py:228-232). Avoid any phrasing like 'predicts approval probability' or 'expected return' without the benchmark/heuristic caveat.

**Real today:**
  - Clinical-phase LOA priors are accurately transcribed from BIO/Informa CDSR 2011-2020: phase1 0.079, phase2 0.151, phase3 0.460, filed 0.906 (cad/cost_benefit.py:38-41) match the published all-disease phase-to-approval success rates (7.9% / 15.1% / ~46% / 90.6%).
  - Clinical per-phase out-of-pocket costs are accurately transcribed from DiMasi et al. 2016: phase1 $25M, phase2 $60M, phase3 $255M (cad/cost_benefit.py:47-49) match DiMasi's $25.3M / $58.6M / $255.4M.
  - The arithmetic itself is simple and correct: remaining cost = sum of forward per-phase costs x modality multiplier (cad/cost_benefit.py:80-81); peak revenue = incidence x price x penetration (cad/cost_benefit.py:85); BCR = risk-adjusted profit / cost (cad/cost_benefit.py:90).
  - The code is unusually candid in-line: it flags MODALITY_MULT as 'HEURISTIC, not fetched data' (cad/cost_benefit.py:54-55), the oncology factor as an 'editable, transparent adjustment' (cad/cost_benefit.py:66-68), and prints every assumption alongside the result (cad/cost_benefit.py:112-122, 143-147) with a 'not financial advice, not a valuation, not a clinical claim' disclaimer (cad/cost_benefit.py:146-147).
  - The triage ranking weights are explicitly labeled a 'TRANSPARENT, NON-VALIDATED ranking HEURISTIC ... not fitted to any benchmark ... never a prediction of efficacy' (cad/virtual_triage.py:228-232), and the underlying inputs (pChEMBL, QED, Ro5 violations) are real ChEMBL-computed values.
**Stub/wrapper/unvalidated:**
  - Preclinical LOA 0.060 (cad/cost_benefit.py:37) is an invented round number — BIO CDSR is a clinical-phase (Phase I onward) report and contains no preclinical-to-approval rate; the comment '(overall, BIO 2011-2020)' on line 35 implies a source it does not have.
  - Modality multipliers (cad/cost_benefit.py:56-63) are hand-set constants applied to cost only — not to LOA and not to time — so e.g. CAR-T gets 1.8x cost but the identical approval probability and timeline as a small molecule.
  - ONCOLOGY_LOA_FACTOR 0.65 (cad/cost_benefit.py:68) is a single flat multiplier applied across all phases (cad/cost_benefit.py:76); it is not a sourced constant and ignores that the oncology/all-area LOA ratio varies sharply by phase in the BIO data.
  - gross_margin 0.80 (cad/cost_benefit.py:86) is a flat heuristic applied to all modalities including small molecules and cell/gene therapies whose real COGS differ greatly.
  - 'filed' cost $20M and all per-phase durations (cad/cost_benefit.py:45-51) are rough planning estimates, not the DiMasi/Wong figures; DiMasi's preclinical out-of-pocket cost is far larger than the $10M on line 46.
  - Verdict thresholds 5 / 1.5 / 0.8 (cad/cost_benefit.py:92-99) are arbitrary cutoffs with no cited basis.
  - The entire 'feasibility verdict' is target- and molecule-agnostic: probability_of_approval (cad/cost_benefit.py:76) depends only on phase, modality flag, and oncology flag, so any two programs at the same phase/modality receive an identical verdict.

## Core CADD pipeline (cad/run_pipeline.py, target_report.py, virtual_triage.py, binding_site.py, fetch_structure.py)

**Summary:** The pipeline is genuinely wired to live public APIs (ChEMBL, UniProt, RCSB, AlphaFold) and computes real things from real data — it is not a template generator, and it is unusually honest about its heuristics (scoring weights are explicitly flagged as non-validated). But the scientific core has several real CADD correctness problems. The docking box is the bounding envelope of whatever hetero group has the most atoms in a single PDB — not necessarily the co-crystal inhibitor — and its center (mean of atoms) and size (min/max extent) are computed inconsistently. The three resolution paths (ChEMBL target, UniProt entry, best PDB) are independent fuzzy lookups that can land on different proteins, and only virtual_triage does exact gene-symbol disambiguation; target_report and fetch_structure do not. The "potent activities" count applies no potency floor, and the ligand triage scans only an arbitrary, unordered first slice of ChEMBL activities while pooling IC50/Ki/Kd/EC50 and keeping the single most favorable value per molecule. Useful as cited research orientation; misleading if read as a validated potency/druggability ranking.

**Usefulness:** For a researcher or med-chemist this is a legitimate, cited starting point: it pulls real ChEMBL ligands with SMILES and links, real UniProt/PDB metadata, and a real structure file, all keyless and reproducible. It is NOT safe to read as a validated ranking. A med-chemist would be misled by (a) 'potent activities = N' which has no potency floor and counts records not compounds; (b) the 'top candidates' which are best-of-an-arbitrary-4000-record slice (no order_by) with the single most favorable IC50/Ki/Kd/EC50 kept per molecule; and (c) the docking box, which can be built on a glycan/cofactor/peptide rather than the inhibitor and whose center/size are geometrically inconsistent — feeding it straight into Vina (as SUMMARY.md instructs) could dock into the wrong region or clip the pocket. The cross-stage resolution mismatch means the dossier, the ligands, and the structure are not guaranteed to be the same protein. No oncologist/clinical use; it is explicitly research orientation and should stay there.

**README notes:** README.md:118 'Docking box computed from the co-crystal ligand envelope' should be softened to 'largest non-solvent hetero ligand (may not be the inhibitor; verify pocket)'. README.md:54 'ChEMBL potent activities = 21342' is inaccurate — there is no potency threshold (only pchembl non-null) and it counts activity records, not potent ligands; rename to 'ChEMBL activities with a pChEMBL value'. README.md:157/191 imply the docking box is reliable for the intended pocket; add the caveat that the box derives from whichever PDB has best resolution (often apo) and from max-atom-count ligand selection, and that the box ligand is unrelated to the triaged hits. A 'Known limitations' doc should also state: target/UniProt/PDB are resolved by three independent fuzzy lookups that can disagree; ligand scan is an unordered first-N slice (not globally most potent); IC50/Ki/Kd/EC50 are pooled and the most favorable value per molecule is kept; the composite 'score' is an unvalidated heuristic of arbitrary constants.

**Real today:**
  - Live, keyless data pulls from real public APIs with correct endpoints: ChEMBL activity/molecule/mechanism/target_search (virtual_triage.py:43, target_report.py:32), UniProt search with reviewed+human+gene/protein filters (target_report.py:79, fetch_structure.py:55), RCSB file download and AlphaFold API version resolution (fetch_structure.py:32-34, 76-95).
  - Real PDB ATOM/HETATM column parsing by fixed-width offsets for ligand coordinates (binding_site.py:57-76) — genuine geometry from the actual structure file, not a placeholder.
  - pChEMBL is correctly described as -log10(molar potency) and used as the potency axis (virtual_triage.py:8, 367); RDKit ECFP4/Morgan r=2 Tanimoto similarity is a real, standard fingerprint when RDKit is present (virtual_triage.py:251-266).
  - virtual_triage.resolve_target does real exact gene-symbol disambiguation to avoid ChEMBL text-relevance picking an interactor/substring (e.g. AKT1 vs AKT1S1, AURKA vs AURKAIP1) (virtual_triage.py:81-101) — a genuine, well-reasoned correctness guard.
  - AlphaFold model version is resolved live rather than hardcoded, with newest-first fallback (fetch_structure.py:76-95).
  - The scoring weights are explicitly and prominently labeled as a transparent non-validated heuristic, not fetched data (virtual_triage.py:228-232), and SUMMARY.md flags cost-benefit as target-independent (run_pipeline.py:241-243).
  - Pipeline refuses to emit a feasibility verdict when the target cannot be resolved, rather than fabricating one (run_pipeline.py:74-93).
**Stub/wrapper/unvalidated:**
  - RDKit-dependent steps (similarity, PAINS/Brenk liabilities, SDF, extra descriptors) only run if RDKit is installed; otherwise silently skipped with a SUMMARY note (run_pipeline.py:101-109, 224-226; virtual_triage.py:47-54, 292-298).
  - Docking itself is not run — stage 6 (cad/dock.py / AutoDock Vina) is only a printed command string; the pipeline never validates the box against a real dock (run_pipeline.py:250-256, binding_site.py:105-107).
  - cost_benefit P(approval) and benefit/cost are target-independent constants from a fixed BIO/Informa prior, not computed from this target (run_pipeline.py:159-164); provenance source_url is called with entry=None (run_pipeline.py:161).
  - Box ligand selection has no relevance model — it is purely max-atom-count over hetero groups not in a hand-curated IGNORE set (binding_site.py:31-35, 74).
**Inaccuracies:**

      reality:  largest_ligand picks the single hetero residue with the most atoms (binding_site.py:74 `key = max(groups, key=lambda k: len(groups[k]))`).   - [MEDIUM|SPEC-MISMATCH] Box center (mean of atoms) and box size (min/max extent) are computed on inconsistent bases
      claim:    A center+size that encloses the ligand for AutoDock Vina (binding_site.py:8-9, 79-83).

## Verification & provenance system (cad/verify.py, cad/provenance.py, cad/test_provenance.py)

**Summary:** The verification spine is real and better than typical "trust me" provenance: verify.py genuinely re-fetches ChEMBL/UniProt counts, does an independent raw-HTTP SMILES byte-equality pull, and deterministically recomputes cost_benefit, the docking box, PAINS/Brenk/SA liabilities, and the triage score, failing CI on mismatch. But the marketing ("every figure re-pulled from source", "fabrication impossible to hide", "independently re-verifiable") overstates it in five concrete ways: (1) most numeric columns in hits.csv (pChEMBL potency, QED, Ro5, MW, alogp, similarity) are NEVER re-fetched — the score recompute reads them straight from the same row it is checking, so an internally-consistent fabrication passes; (2) DRIFT silently blesses any count change up to max(2, 10%) and still prints "No fabrications"; (3) the SMILES check only proves the SMILES matches the chembl_id in the row, not that the molecule is actually a target hit, so a real-but-wrong compound passes; (4) wrong-but-reproducible queries pass by design, and "potent_activity_records" is itself a mislabeled query; (5) provenance.py's "model origin impossible" is just a string-enum check on a label — it never verifies the value actually came from the source, and verify.py never even reads provenance.json. So the system proves reproducibility/non-tampering of a subset of figures, not correctness, and not freedom from model influence.

**Usefulness:** For its actual target users — med-chemists and computational researchers using this as an OSINT triage front-end — the verifier is genuinely useful as a tamper-detector and reproducibility gate: it will catch hand-edited SMILES, a doctored score column, an edited cost_benefit verdict, or a fabricated docking box, and it forces an openable URL next to every count. That is more than most pipelines offer. Where it would mislead: a reader who believes the README's "every figure re-pulled" / "fabrication impossible to hide" will over-trust the potency and drug-likeness numbers in hits.csv, which are taken on faith from the file (only SMILES and the derived score are checked, and the score is checked against those same unverified inputs). It would also mislead anyone treating a PASS as evidence the query is CORRECT — a mislabeled "potent_activity_records" count (no potency/assay filter) reproduces and passes. No clinician or patient should touch this, and the repo says so.
